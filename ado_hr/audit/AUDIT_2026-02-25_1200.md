### 4.2 Detailed Findings

#### Finding 1: No Story Point Estimation (CRITICAL)

**Issue:** Zero of 30 work items have story point estimates.
**SAFe Standard:** All stories committed to an iteration must be estimated using story points to enable velocity tracking, capacity planning, and predictability metrics.
**Impact:** Without estimates, the team cannot measure velocity, cannot forecast future capacity, and cannot make data-driven commitments during PI Planning.
**Recommendation:** Conduct estimation sessions (Planning Poker) for all iteration backlog items. Use relative sizing (1, 2, 3, 5, 8, 13) based on complexity, effort, and uncertainty.

#### Finding 2: No Team Capacity Configured (CRITICAL)

**Issue:** The Azure DevOps capacity configuration shows zero capacity assigned for Iteration 6.4.
**SAFe Standard:** Teams must define capacity (in hours or story points) for each iteration to enable realistic commitment during Iteration Planning.
**Impact:** No ability to assess if the team is over- or under-committed. No burndown tracking possible.
**Recommendation:** Configure individual capacity for each team member, accounting for meetings, PTO, and other non-development time.

#### Finding 3: Single Team Member — Bus Factor of 1 (CRITICAL)

**Issue:** All 30 work items are assigned exclusively to Almera Kleer Tayao. No other team members are visible in the iteration.
**SAFe Standard:** Agile teams are cross-functional, with 5-11 members who collectively possess all skills needed to deliver value. SAFe explicitly warns against single-person "teams."
**Impact:** Single point of failure for all HR operations. No collaboration, no knowledge sharing, no peer review. If this person is unavailable, all 30 items are blocked.
**Recommendation:** Either expand the team to include additional HR members, or restructure the work to be manageable by one person by reducing WIP and carrying fewer items per iteration.

#### Finding 4: 76.7% of Items Not Started at Day 2 (HIGH)

**Issue:** 23 of 30 items remain in "New" state on Day 2 of a 14-day iteration.
**SAFe Standard:** By the end of Day 2, the team should have started all high-priority items and have a clear execution plan.
**Impact:** Risk of large batch of incomplete work at iteration end, creating carryover and unpredictability.
**Recommendation:** Prioritize the backlog and limit committed items to what can realistically be completed. Move remaining items back to the backlog.

#### Finding 5: Feature-Story Hierarchy Broken (HIGH)

**Issue:** Two critical hierarchy problems:
- **9 orphan stories (30%)** have no parent Feature, breaking traceability
- **Feature #191713** (Employees 2025 Annual Evaluation) is **Closed** but has 2 child stories in **New** state
**SAFe Standard:** Every story must trace to a Feature, and Feature/Story states must be consistent.
**Recommendation:** Link all orphan stories to appropriate Features. Reopen Feature #191713 or move its child stories to the correct parent.

#### Finding 6: No Acceptance Criteria (HIGH)

**Issue:** Stories lack acceptance criteria (Definition of Done at the story level).
**SAFe Standard:** Each user story must have clear, testable acceptance criteria before being committed to an iteration.
**Recommendation:** Add acceptance criteria to every story using Given/When/Then format or a checklist.

#### Finding 7: Stories Not in INVEST Format (MEDIUM)

**Issue:** Most "stories" are task-level items (e.g., "APE - Ates, Jerlyn", "Exit Clearance") rather than proper user stories.
**SAFe Standard:** Stories should follow the INVEST model (Independent, Negotiable, Valuable, Estimable, Small, Testable) and use the format: "As a [role], I want [capability], so that [benefit]."
**Recommendation:** Rewrite stories to express value from the user/stakeholder perspective.

#### Finding 8: No WIP Limits (MEDIUM)

**Issue:** 4 items active simultaneously for a single-person team with no WIP limits set.
**SAFe Standard:** Teams should limit WIP to optimize flow and reduce context switching.
**Recommendation:** Set WIP limit of 1-2 for a single-person team. Use a Kanban-style flow to pull work sequentially.

#### Finding 9: No Clear PI Objectives Alignment (MEDIUM)

**Issue:** The iteration mixes 8 different Features spanning evaluation, recruitment, offboarding, training, medical, and admin — with no clear PI objective linking them.
**SAFe Standard:** Iteration work should directly support committed PI Objectives.
**Recommendation:** Define 2-3 clear iteration goals that map to PI Objectives.

#### Finding 10: No Iteration Goal Defined (MEDIUM)

**Issue:** No iteration goal or theme is set for Iteration 6.4.
**SAFe Standard:** Each iteration should have a clear, measurable iteration goal that the team commits to.
**Recommendation:** Define a focused iteration goal (e.g., "Complete all offboarding tasks for Ike Lloyd Yana and finalize Bubble Developer hiring").
