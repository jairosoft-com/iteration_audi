### 7.2 Key Patterns Identified

1. **Systemic non-execution:** Three audits over two days show zero work item state changes, zero capacity planning, and zero sprint goal setting. This is not a timing issue — it reflects an absence of the Iteration Planning ceremony.

2. **Recommendation non-adoption:** 12 recommendations were made across the first two audits (R1–R6 per audit). None have been acted upon. This suggests the audit findings are not reaching decision-makers or there is no mechanism to assign and track corrective actions.

3. **Persistent DoR violations:** Item #199978 has been flagged in all three audits for missing Description and Acceptance Criteria. The responsible party (Jerlyn Ates) has not been notified or has not acted.

4. **Single-team-member risk persists:** While the backlog shows items assigned to Jaszmeine, Aldrin, and Jerlyn, none of these assignments have translated into sprint work. The team is not functioning as a unit.

5. **Aging work items:** #193129 (Attorneys Overview REVAMP) has been open for 6.5 months. Per SAFe flow metrics, items exceeding their target cycle time should be escalated or broken down further.
