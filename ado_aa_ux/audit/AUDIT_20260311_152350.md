### 8.3 Key Patterns Identified

1. **Systemic non-execution confirmed:** Four audits over three days show zero work item state changes, zero capacity planning, zero sprint goal setting, and zero recommendations acted upon. This is a confirmed process breakdown — not a timing issue, not a data lag, but an absence of the Iteration Planning and execution ceremonies.

2. **Recommendation non-adoption is total:** 35 recommendations have been issued across three audits with a 0% action rate. This suggests either: (a) audit findings are not reaching decision-makers, (b) there is no mechanism to assign and track corrective actions, or (c) the team lacks the authority or capacity to act on recommendations.

3. **Iteration path management gap:** Items are assigned to expired sprints (6.2, 6.4) and future PIs (PI7) but not to the active sprint (6.5). This indicates the team is not performing backlog grooming or sprint planning as part of their regular cadence.

4. **Item #193129 approaching critical age:** At 168 days (5.5 months), this item is a significant flow metric outlier. Per SAFe, items exceeding their target cycle time by this margin should be escalated, broken down, or formally deprioritized. All 4 child tasks have remained in "New" state since creation.

5. **Team dysfunction pattern:** Despite having 3 team members visible in the backlog (Jaszmeine, Aldrin, Jerlyn) and a PM (Karl), the team has not held a single planning session, standup, or retrospective across the audit window. This suggests structural team dysfunction beyond individual accountability.
