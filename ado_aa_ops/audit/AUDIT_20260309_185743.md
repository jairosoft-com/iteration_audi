## 6. SAFe Compliance Findings

### 6.1 Iteration Planning (Score: 30% — Critical)

**Finding:** Only 1 of 11 backlog items is committed to Iteration 6.5. The team appears to have not conducted a proper Iteration Planning event.

**SAFe Standard:** Teams should collaboratively plan iteration scope during Iteration Planning, committing to a set of work items that match their capacity and velocity.

**Recommendation:** Conduct an Iteration Planning session immediately. Review the 10 unassigned backlog items and commit appropriate ones to Iteration 6.5.

### 6.2 Team Capacity (Score: 0% — Critical)

**Finding:** No team capacity has been configured for Iteration 6.5.

**SAFe Standard:** Teams must set individual capacity (hours per day, days off) to enable realistic planning and workload balancing.

**Recommendation:** Karl (PM) should configure team capacity for all members (Jerlyn, Mary, Axle, Karl) in ADO immediately.

### 6.3 Story Points / Estimation (Score: 0% — Critical)

**Finding:** Zero story points are estimated across all 11 backlog items.

**SAFe Standard:** All work items should be estimated using story points to enable velocity tracking, capacity planning, and predictability metrics.

**Recommendation:** Conduct a backlog refinement session to estimate all items. Even Spikes should be time-boxed and sized.

### 6.4 Definition of Ready (DoR) Compliance (Score: 20% — Critical)

**Finding:** Only 1 item (195253) is in "Ready" state. Most items lack clear acceptance criteria in their descriptions. The Defect (199547) in Iteration 6.5 has a description but no formal acceptance criteria.

**SAFe Standard:** Items must meet the DoR (Description + Acceptance Criteria) before being pulled into an iteration.

**Recommendation:** Add acceptance criteria to all backlog items, especially 199547 before work begins.

### 6.5 Work Item Balance (Score: 40% — Warning)

**Finding:** 70% of backlog items are Spikes. No User Stories exist on the backlog. Spikes should produce Stories as output, but none are visible.

**SAFe Standard:** A healthy backlog should contain a mix of Stories, Enablers, and limited Spikes. Spikes should be time-boxed and result in actionable Stories.

**Recommendation:** Review completed Spike output and convert findings into User Stories. The team should transition from research to delivery.

### 6.6 Backlog Refinement (Score: 30% — Critical)

**Finding:** Several items reference previous iterations (e.g., "IT 6.3", "5.3") but remain Active, suggesting carryover without re-evaluation. Items from earlier iterations have not been updated or re-prioritized.

**SAFe Standard:** Backlog refinement should occur regularly to ensure items are current, properly prioritized, and ready for upcoming iterations.

**Recommendation:** Schedule a backlog refinement session to re-evaluate carryover items, update priorities, and ensure items are groomed for the remaining iterations in PI6.
