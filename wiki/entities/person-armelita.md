## Delivery pattern

Armelita is both the **PM** (plans and grooms the iteration) and **PO** (accepts stories and defines DoR). In delivery she's also the **primary IC**, routinely carrying **70–80%+ of story points** on the team. This is a concentration risk similar in shape — but larger in scope — to [[entities/person-mark-colina]]'s bus-factor-1 on Administration. The difference: Armelita has secondary contributors (Teofilo, Grace, Samantha), but their allocations are small enough that she remains the critical path.

### Productive patterns cited

- **Late-sprint burst + proactive carry-over.** She has a repeating pattern of closing a cluster of items near sprint end AND proactively moving remaining items to the next iteration's backlog. Example: [[summaries/audit-ado-jit-20260322-2329]] — "Armelita proactively moved 3 items (5 SP) to Iter 6.6" after finishing her own items.
- **Scope discipline.** When items are added mid-iteration, they typically get added to her (e.g., [[summaries/audit-ado-jit-20260304-2209]]: "#200105 added late to Armelita despite her carrying the bulk already"). She absorbs rather than deflects.

## Risk signals (from the audit chain)

1. **Structural over-concentration** — routinely 70–80%+ of SP. If Armelita is unavailable, JIT delivery halts.
2. **PM + PO + IC triple-hat** — she grooms, accepts, and delivers. This is unusual for SAFe and creates role-conflict risk (who rejects her own stories?).
3. **Sprint-risk escalation when she slows** — e.g., [[summaries/audit-ado-jit-20260320-0800]] "Armelita and Samantha remain at 8% and 0% completion respectively — sprint risk" when her pace drops mid-sprint.

## Recommended actions (feed into PI7.2 planning)

1. **Rebalance commitment** away from Armelita-dominance. If Teofilo has unused capacity, move at least 2 items to him at planning time.
2. **Separate PM and PO responsibilities** — even informally. Self-accepting one's own stories is a rubric-level concern and a practical conflict-of-interest.
3. **Monitor Armelita's bus-factor** explicitly in portfolio dashboard — it's structurally similar to Mark Colina's on [[entities/team-ado-admin]] and belongs in the same bus-factor-1 view per [[synthesis/top-compliance-issues]] rank #4.
